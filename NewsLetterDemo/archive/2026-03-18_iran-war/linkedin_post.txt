18 days into the US-Israel war on Iran, and the most important story isn't the strike count.

It's the strategic mismatch.

The US and Israel want to destroy Iran's nuclear capability and force regime accountability. Iran wants one thing: to survive long enough for US political will to collapse.

Iran has been preparing for this scenario for 40 years — proxy networks, missile arsenals, Hormuz leverage. Its military is weaker. Its strategy is not.

Finland's FM said this week that NATO is 'not obliged to help Trump in Iran.' This is a coalition of two prosecuting a war against a country that has spent decades preparing to outlast exactly this kind of coalition.

The question isn't whether Iran can be defeated militarily. The question is whether anyone has a clear theory of how this ends.

Day 18 suggests the answer is no — on either side.
